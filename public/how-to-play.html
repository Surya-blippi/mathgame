HOW TO PLAY LEARN FIRE

Your complete guide to mastering the educational FPS experience

What is Learn Fire?

Learn Fire is a browser-based first-person shooter where every bullet is an answer. Instead of mindless action, you solve math problems, answer English questions, and tackle general knowledge quizzes to defeat waves of robot enemies. It runs directly in your web browser on both desktop and mobile devices — no downloads or installations required.

The core loop is simple: robots approach you, each displaying a question. Their body parts show different answer choices. Shoot the correct answer to eliminate the enemy and earn points. Answer wrong and the robot keeps coming. Survive wave after wave as questions get progressively harder.

Getting Started

Visit learnfire.live in any modern browser (Chrome, Safari, Firefox, or Edge). The game loads instantly. On the start screen you will see options to choose your game mode and weapon before jumping into the action.

  1. Choose your game mode — Select from Math, English, or Quiz mode depending on what subject you want to practice.
  2. Pick your weapon — Each gun has a different feel. Try them all to find your favorite.
  3. Press START MISSION — You will be dropped into a 3D arena with robots approaching from all directions.
  4. Answer questions by shooting — Read the question, find the correct answer on the robot, and shoot that body part.
  5. Survive as long as you can — Each wave brings more robots and harder questions.

Desktop Controls

W A S D Move forward, left, backward, and right
Mouse Look around the arena
Left Click Shoot at the targeted answer
Space Jump over obstacles
1 2 3 Switch between weapons

On desktop, the game uses pointer lock for smooth mouse-look controls. Click the game window to activate pointer lock. Press Escape to release the cursor if you need to pause or access the menu.

Mobile Controls

Learn Fire is fully optimized for mobile devices in landscape mode. The touch controls are designed to feel natural and responsive.

Left Joystick Virtual analog stick for movement
Right Area Swipe Swipe to look around
Tap Tap on the screen to shoot
Jump Button Blue button in the bottom-right corner

For the best mobile experience, rotate your device to landscape orientation before starting the game. The game will prompt you to rotate if it detects portrait mode.

Game Modes Explained

Math Mode

Robots display arithmetic problems like addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. Early waves start with simple single-digit calculations and gradually progress to multi-digit operations, fractions, and more complex equations. This mode is excellent for students practicing mental math and building speed with calculations.

English Mode

Test your vocabulary, grammar, and language skills. Questions range from identifying correct spellings and definitions to grammar rules and sentence completion. This mode helps learners expand their vocabulary and reinforce proper English usage in an engaging way.

Quiz Mode

A mixed bag of general knowledge questions spanning science, history, geography, and more. This mode challenges your breadth of knowledge and keeps you on your toes with varied question types. Great for trivia lovers and students preparing for exams.

Weapons and Shooting

Learn Fire features multiple weapons, each with unique characteristics. Experiment with different guns to find the one that suits your play style. Some fire faster, while others have a more satisfying visual impact.

When you see a robot, read the question displayed above it. The robot's body is divided into sections — head, chest, and legs — each showing a different answer choice. Aim carefully and shoot the section displaying the correct answer to score a hit. Headshots on the correct answer award bonus points.

Scoring and Waves

Your score increases with each correct answer. Consecutive correct answers build a combo multiplier that significantly boosts your score. Missing an answer or getting hit by a robot resets your combo. The goal is to maintain a high streak while surviving as many waves as possible.

Each wave introduces more robots that move faster and present harder questions. Between waves you get a brief moment to prepare. Your highest score is saved locally so you can always try to beat your personal best.

Tips and Strategies

  • Keep moving — Standing still makes you an easy target. Strafe left and right while you solve questions.
  • Prioritize close enemies — Robots that are closer deal damage sooner. Take them out first before targeting distant ones.
  • Read before you shoot — Take a moment to read the question and find the right answer. A missed shot wastes time and resets your combo.
  • Use headshots — If you are confident in the answer, aim for the head to earn bonus points.
  • Practice regularly — Like any skill, both your gaming reflexes and academic knowledge improve with practice. Play a few rounds daily for the best results.
  • Try all modes — Switching between Math, English, and Quiz mode keeps the experience fresh and exercises different parts of your brain.

System Requirements

Learn Fire runs in any modern web browser that supports WebGL. No special hardware or software is needed.

  • Desktop: Chrome 80+, Firefox 75+, Safari 14+, or Edge 80+
  • Mobile: iOS Safari 14+ or Android Chrome 80+
  • Internet: A stable connection for the initial load (the game is lightweight at under 5 MB)

For the smoothest experience on mobile, close other browser tabs and apps to free up memory.
